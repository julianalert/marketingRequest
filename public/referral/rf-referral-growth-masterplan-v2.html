01 — The opportunity

You're already getting referrals.
You're just not capturing them.

The most important finding from four months of sales data is that a referral channel exists and is functioning — completely without a program, incentives, or infrastructure. Building one won't create new behaviour. It will 10× behaviour that's already happening.

What's happening right now

Regen Financial has already sent SquidRouter and is actively trying to onboard Bankless — with zero formal agreement. Withum told clients about RF after a single demo. Propeller Industries, with 40 clients including Solana and Gitcoin, discussed a referral agreement and left without one. Anchor Labs asked for a referral program and got a conversation instead of a contract. Every week this gap exists, you leave warm introductions on the table.

Why this channel will outperform all others

A fractional CFO firm that advises 20 web3 companies is not a lead — it is a distribution channel. When they recommend a tool, their clients adopt it without a sales cycle. The trust is pre-built. Referral-sourced deals close faster, churn less, and require zero paid media. For a B2B fintech in a trust-sensitive niche, this is the most efficient acquisition channel available.

"The fastest path to 400 paid customers is not a bigger ad budget — it is formalising the referral network that is already warming up to you, and simultaneously building a systematic machine to find and sign 100 new partners that don't know you exist yet."

08 — New: Cold partner discovery

Why the warm pipeline
is not enough

The 15 warm partners in your pipeline are your fastest wins — but they represent a fraction of the addressable partner universe. To go from 200 to 400 customers on the referral channel alone, you need a systematic machine to find, qualify, and sign partners who have never heard of Request Finance.

The math forces your hand

Even in the target model, your warm pipeline delivers ~139 new customers. To hit 400 reliably — accounting for churn, slow conversions, and partners who sign but never activate — you need a cold pipeline producing an additional 60–80 new customers in parallel. That requires signing 20–30 net-new partners who don't know you today.

Cold partners often outperform warm ones

Warm partners know RF casually. A cold partner who is specifically recruited because their entire client base matches your ICP — for example, a web3 accounting firm in Singapore with 25 DeFi protocol clients — will have a higher conversion rate than a general advisor who mentions RF occasionally. Precision targeting beats warmth.

"Think of cold partner prospecting the way you think of cold sales prospecting — but with a 10× multiplier. One signed partner replaces 20–40 individual sales calls. The effort per customer acquired is a fraction of any other channel."
Market size

How many potential partners exist?

Globally: 800+ web3-focused accounting and CFO firms. 200+ digital asset law firms. 500+ blockchain infrastructure companies. 300+ DAO tooling and treasury platforms. 150+ crypto media and education properties. The addressable partner universe is in the thousands.

Realistic target

What you need to sign

To produce 60–80 new customers: 15–20 new Tier 1/2 partners (at 5 intros/year each, 35% conversion). Or 3–4 new Tier 3 ecosystem partners (at 30 intros/year each, 25% conversion). Both together is the most reliable path.

Time investment

What cold prospecting costs

At 40 outreach attempts per week (4 hours/week), with a 15% response rate and 30% close rate from responses, you sign 1–2 new partners per week. In 12 weeks: 12–24 net-new partners. This is achievable with one dedicated resource — a partner development manager or a dedicated 20% of a BDR's time.

12 — Cold outreach playbook

How to approach partners
who don't know you exist

Cold partner outreach is different from cold sales outreach. You are not selling them a product — you are offering them a revenue stream for work they are already doing. The frame is "I want to pay you" not "I want to sell you."

Sequence 1 For web3 accounting firms (Type A) — cold LinkedIn or email
MESSAGE 1 · DAY 1 · 50 WORDS MAX
Subject: Quick question — do your clients use any spend management tools?

Hi [Name], I lead partnerships at Request Finance — we're a spend management and crypto payment platform used by 200+ web3 companies.

We just launched a partner program for accounting firms: you refer clients, we pay 20% of their first-year subscription. Worth 15 minutes?
MESSAGE 2 · DAY 5 · IF NO REPLY
Hi [Name], just bumping this. We work with companies like [relevant client from their niche — e.g. "DeFi protocols in the Ethereum ecosystem"]. Happy to send the one-pager if useful — no call needed to get started.
MESSAGE 3 · DAY 12 · FINAL
Last note — if this isn't a fit right now I completely understand. I'll leave the link here in case it's useful later: [partner page]. We've already signed several web3 accounting firms this quarter and the program is live.
Sequence 2 For infrastructure / ecosystem partners (Type B) — LinkedIn
MESSAGE 1 · DAY 1
Hi [Name], I noticed [Company]'s users overlap heavily with ours — both serving web3 teams managing treasury and payments. We're building co-marketing partnerships with infrastructure tools this quarter. The pitch: you mention RF to your users, we mention [product] to ours, and we split revenue on joint customers. Worth a call?
MESSAGE 2 · DAY 6
Following up — specifically on [a client they'd recognise, e.g. "we work with several Safe multisig teams who would benefit from tighter [Company] integration"]. A 20-minute call to explore the overlap might be worth it.
MESSAGE 3 · DAY 14
Last touch. Here's a 2-minute overview of what the partnership looks like in practice: [link to partnership page]. If the timing is off, I'll reconnect in Q3.
Sequence 3 For law firms and advisory firms (Type C) — email

We've found that law firms and advisors working in the web3 space often get asked "what should we use for crypto payments?" by clients. We've put together a simple kit — a one-pager and a referral link — so you have a good answer ready and earn a commission if a client signs up. No agreement needed to get started. Interested in the kit?
MESSAGE 2 · DAY 7
Just circling back. Happy to send the one-pager without any commitment — takes 10 minutes to review and you'll at least have a good answer for the next client who asks about crypto payments.
Sequence 4 For newsletter authors and community managers (Type D)
MESSAGE 1 · DAY 1
Subject: Partnership for [newsletter name] readers

Hi [Name], I read [newsletter name] regularly — especially your piece on [specific topic]. Your readers are exactly who Request Finance is built for: finance managers at web3 companies managing cross-border payments and crypto treasury.

MESSAGE 2 · DAY 8
Also happy to just do a sponsored mention to test fit before committing to anything formal. We pay for newsletter sponsorships too — whatever works best for your format.

Cold outreach rules that matter

Personalisation is not optional

Every message in sequence 1 and 2 must include one specific reference to the partner's work, clients, or content. A generic blast will get 2% response rate. A personalised one gets 15–20%. The difference is research — spend 3 minutes on their LinkedIn before hitting send.

3 touches maximum, then move on

If a partner doesn't respond after 3 messages over 12 days, they are not interested right now. Archive them and re-approach in 90 days when you have something new to say (a new feature, a case study, a client they know). Never send a 4th follow-up in the same sequence.

Volume is the game

At a 15% response rate and 30% close rate from responses: 40 outreach/week → 6 responses → 2 signed partners/week → 24 partners in 12 weeks. The funnel only works if you maintain volume. Build a Notion or Airtable tracker so nothing falls through the cracks.

13 — Qualify & score partners

Not every partner is worth
the same amount of time

Use this scoring framework to prioritise which cold prospects get personal outreach from the CMO or a senior rep, and which ones get a standard sequence.

Partner quality score (0–100)

Client portfolio size & relevance 0–30 points

30 pts: 20+ web3 clients actively managed · 15 pts: 5–20 clients · 5 pts: <5 clients or mixed portfolio

Tool recommendation behaviour 0–25 points

25 pts: actively mentions specific tools to clients · 12 pts: occasionally recommends · 0 pts: no evidence

Gap in current payment tool 0–20 points

20 pts: no crypto payments tool in their stack · 10 pts: uses competitor · 0 pts: already recommends RF or direct competitor

Distribution / reach 0–15 points

15 pts: newsletter, podcast, or community >1,000 followers · 7 pts: active content creator · 0 pts: no distribution

Geographic overlap with RF pipeline 0–10 points

10 pts: operates in US, UK, Singapore, UAE, France · 5 pts: EMEA/APAC generally · 0 pts: regions with no RF presence

75+

50–74
Good fit — standard outreach sequence (BDR)

Solid partner potential. Use sequence 1 or 2 depending on type. Offer Tier 1 first, upgrade to Tier 2 if they bring volume. These will form the majority of your signed partners.
