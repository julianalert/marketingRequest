01 — Executive summary

What the sales floor is telling us

Four months of meeting data reveals five macro-level signals that should reshape marketing priorities immediately.

The biggest shift

Non-web3 companies are now entering the pipeline organically. A Brazilian music platform (Sua Música), a US real estate group, a UK logistics holding, a Virginia Airbnb operator, and a global affiliate network all appeared in the last 4 months. The addressable market is expanding beyond crypto-native companies, and our marketing doesn't reflect this yet.

The biggest threat

Dakota is being mentioned by leads as a free alternative. Slash is undercutting on offramp fees. Reap is expanding. Multiple leads cite "pricing too expensive" as the primary blocker. The market is commoditizing C2F and basic AP features. We need to shift positioning toward the full-stack value (AP + AR + Cards + GUSD + Approvals) before competitors close the gap.

Five macro signals

Signal 1

Global USD Account is the magnet

40%+ of new meetings mention GUSD interest. It's the #1 inbound driver and the feature competitors don't have.

Signal 2

Cards are table stakes

35+ leads explicitly asked for crypto cards. Leads are actively using Reap, Cypher, RedotPay, and Ether.fi. Delay = churn risk.

Signal 3

Web2 is coming

12+ meetings with non-crypto businesses. Cross-border pain + stablecoin curiosity = new TAM. Current site repels them.

Signal 4

Pricing is the #1 objection

C2F fees "too high" mentioned in 20+ meetings. Dakota, Slash, OTC desks all cited as cheaper. Value-based pricing narrative needed.

Signal 5

Accounting integrations unlock deals

QuickBooks, Xero, and NetSuite mentioned in 25+ meetings. Missing integrations = lost deals (Kiln, Flowdesk, P2P).

10 — Website & conversion insights

What the site needs to change

Specific website issues flagged directly or implied by sales conversations.

Conversion blockers found in calls

  • Accounting branding too prominent: Ubyx came expecting an accounting demo because the UI still promotes it heavily despite the sunset plan. This is misleading new leads.
  • No "book a demo" for GUSD: Multiple leads (Accountable, Piertwo, Arbitrum) expressed interest in GUSD but had to be told about it in a call — no discoverable page.
  • Pricing page scares people: C2F rates prominently shown cause sticker shock. Leads cite 0.9% as "too expensive" before understanding the full platform value.
  • No industry/use-case pages: Gaming leads, agencies, and security firms can't find themselves in the product positioning at all.
  • Kiln's CFO never had a proper demo: He uses Request for free (AR) but never understood the full offering — suggesting we don't have a nurture flow for free users.
  • Input Global lead dropped off after hearing "the app wasn't safe": This reputation concern in the Dubai market suggests we need visible security credentials on the site.

What should go on the homepage

  • Global USD Account — front and center. This is the new magnet product.
  • Corporate Cards — "Coming soon" with waitlist. Creates urgency and captures leads.
  • "One platform for all business payments" — leads consistently want to consolidate 3-5 tools into Request.
  • Integration logos — QuickBooks, Xero, Safe, Fireblocks, Metamask, Coinbase Prime (even if some are pending).
  • Trust signals — SOC-2, security certifications, client logos, review badges (G2, Capterra).
  • "Works for crypto-native AND traditional businesses" — explicitly broadening the audience.
  • Quick demo video — Kiln, Flowdesk, and others needed a demo to understand the product. A 2-minute product walkthrough video on the homepage would convert.
11 — SEO strategy implications

Connecting sales data to the SEO strategy

The sales meeting data validates and reshapes several priorities in the SEO strategy document.

✅ Validated by sales data

  • Web3 moat is real: Zero leads mention any competitor in the Web3 finance space. SG Forge, Kiln, Ledger, ADAN CFO Board — all confirm the uncontested position.
  • Comparison pages are critical: "Ramp alternative" and "Brex alternative" keywords match the Ramp mentions in sales calls (8+ leads use Ramp for US, need a crypto alternative).
  • Long-tail keywords work: Leads searching for specific pain points ("crypto payroll compliance", "stablecoin B2B payments") match exactly the low-difficulty keywords in the SEO plan.

🔄 Needs adjustment based on sales data

  • Add "Dakota alternative" and "Bitwage alternative" to the comparison pages list — these are real competitive encounters, not theoretical.
  • Add iGaming/gaming vertical to industry use-case pages — 5+ leads from this sector.
  • Prioritize LATAM/Portuguese content over French/German in the Q4 localization plan — sales data shows LATAM is the hotter market.
  • Add "Global USD Account" keyword cluster — terms like "virtual USD account for crypto companies", "business stablecoin account" should be a new content pillar.
  • Deprioritize pure "spend management" head terms for now — leads are not finding us through these generic terms; they come for specific crypto/stablecoin payment needs.
